A science-education article on the biomedical importance and conservation challenges of horseshoe crabs, published in Roshd Magazine (Persian).
Horseshoe Crabs: Sensitive Detectors of Toxic Bacteria and Key Players in Modern Vaccines and Drugs
Roshd Educational Magazine – Science & Biology Education Section
Original Persian title:
«خرچنگ‌های نعل اسبی – حساس به باکتری‌های سمی و بازیگر مهم در تولید واکسن‌ها و داروهای نوین»
English Summary:
This article introduces the unique biology of horseshoe crabs and explains why their blue blood plays an essential role in global biomedical safety. It describes the Limulus Amebocyte Lysate (LAL) test, a critical method used worldwide to detect bacterial endotoxins in vaccines, injectable drugs, and medical devices. The article also highlights ethical concerns and conservation challenges caused by harvesting wild horseshoe crabs for the pharmaceutical industry, while discussing emerging synthetic alternatives that may reduce reliance on wild populations. Written for students and educators, the article translates a complex biomedical topic into clear, accessible language.
Part of a series of popular-science articles in Roshd Magazine.
